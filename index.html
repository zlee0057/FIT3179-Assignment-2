Exploring the Flow

Income Impact on Migration

With the exception of high-income groups, the individuals from other income groups want to leave their countries.

Middle-income groups seek improved economic prospects and living conditions while low-income migrants often move out of necessity to escape poverty and adverse circumstances.

In the year 2020, however, migration rate took a nosedive with the spread of Covid-19.

Development Dynamics on Migration

Developmental group, which refers to the level of development in a region or country, significantly impacts migration flow.

Individuals from less-developed regions and least-developed regions may migrate to more-developed regions in search of better opportunities, education, and living conditions.

This was near impossible to do, however, in 2020 due to the spread of Covid-19.

What is Migrant Stock?

Migrant Stock is the record of the number of migrants residing in a particular destination country at any point in time. While it is not exactly migration flow, such data can, however, assist in the computation of migration flow estimates. This is especially helpful since accurate data regarding migration flow is much more difficult to obtain compared to data regarding migrant stock.

Influence of Gender on Migration

Gender plays a pivotal role in shaping migrant flows worldwide. Traditional gender roles, labor market dynamics, and societal expectations often lead to distinct migration patterns for men and women.

For instance, men may migrate more for employment opportunities in certain sectors, while women may be driven by family reunification or caregiving roles.

In the population pyramid to the right, you can see that more men (specifically males of age 20 to 49) who have migrated, the majority of which may be assumed to have done so in order to search more opportunities in life.

Migration Flow Across Regions

Migration flow across regions is a complex and dynamic phenomenon driven by a multitude of factors. This movement of people can result in shifts in population demographics, labor force composition, and cultural exchanges, impacting both the origin and destination regions.

In the Sankey Diagram below, you can observe both inter and intra-regional migration patterns estimated using migrant stock. Looking at it in detail, you will notice that countries within Asia are the most popular destinations of migration as it fills up 42.9% of the destination column.
